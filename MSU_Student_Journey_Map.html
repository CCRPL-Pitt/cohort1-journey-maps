⚑ Stage 1 Gap Summary
Preliminary Milestones
Departments no longer receive the Admit List. Silent email transition (personal → MSU) causes missed comms. SOAR date/retention correlation documented but formally unconfirmed.
Financial Access
Scholarship page lacks current info. Housing rates unavailable when applications open. Missouri Access Grant deadline (Feb 1) poorly communicated. Financial literacy scaffolding absent alongside award letter.
Academic Foundation
Math placement policy unclear across departments. Dual-credit students may face locked or duplicate account barriers. SoBL added as credit-conferring assessment.
Advising & Planning
SOAR advisor ≠ permanent advisor. No centralized scheduling. First advising interaction is one-time, high-pressure, no relationship context.
Belonging & Engagement
LLC promotion inconsistent. Commuter students have SOAR session but no equivalent Welcome Week touchpoint. Roommate dependency risk unmanaged.
Health & Basic Needs
CC is proactively present — documentation corrected. Real gap: whether Black, LGBTQ+, and first-gen students are choosing to utilize available touchpoints.
Career & Future Self
Career presence at SOAR is optional. Handshake enrollment on first day of class; ~42% activation rate — majority of students remain passive.

⚑ Stage 2 Gap Summary
Financial Access
30-credit scholarship renewal requirement in structural tension with 14–15 credit first-semester loads. Financial holds and billing complexity minimally documented, with no formal escalation pathway.
Academic Foundation
GEP 101 is 2 credit hours (corrected). IDS 120 is optional. No notification if students drop below 12 credits. Career content in GEP 101 not required — uneven exposure across sections.
Advising & Planning
Decentralized advising continues. ATLAS launching but not at full coverage. Boomer career timeline checklist exists but not formally integrated into advising.
Belonging & Engagement
Commuter students face structural belonging disadvantage. House Calls is one-time, residential-only. Work-study eligibility changes limit on-campus employment access.
Health & Basic Needs
CC proactive (confirmed). Real gap: students who most need support not choosing to utilize touchpoints. Midterms + fall break + drop period coincide — known crisis convergence point, no formal response protocol.
Career & Future Self
Career Center in GEP 101 not required. Handshake activation ~42%. Boomer career timeline checklist not formally integrated into advising appointments.

⚑ Stage 3 Gap Summary
Financial Access
Financial Aid office confirmed: no staffing capacity for proactive individualized advising. Transfer students face aid gap surprises. Emergency financial aid not formally mapped for transfers.
Academic Foundation
Major admission requirements vary widely. Students approaching 75-hr cliff about to lose required advising. Required advising = 75 hours AND 3 advisor releases (corrected).
Advising & Planning
Required advising ends at 75 hrs + 3 releases. No documented post-75-hour model. COB credit-hour reporting model is potential institutional template.
Belonging & Engagement
Transfer orientation first held Jan 2026 — not yet robust. Transfer support concentrated in a single staff role serving ~half the student body (Focus Group, April 2026). Honor society leadership tracking confirmed: ~10% of sophomore org members hold an executive board role (318 of 3,189). Whether honor society invitations reach first-gen and Pell-eligible students who qualify remains open.
Career & Future Self
Résumé, LinkedIn, internship search mapped here — largely student-driven. Unpaid internship trade-off vs. paid employment affects low-income students disproportionately.

⚑ Stage 4 Gap Summary
Financial Access
Scholarships may be exhausted. Transfer students approaching lifetime aid limits. Education Abroad Voucher ($1,000) eliminated — material reduction in access.
Academic Foundation
Upper-division major coursework. No alert if students fall off track. Double-major students may lack advising relationship with second major's department.
Advising & Planning
Most consequential gap in the entire map: required advising ends at 75 hrs. Students entirely self-directed for final 45 credit hours. No graduation readiness check-in mechanism.
Belonging & Engagement
Students expected to hold leadership roles. Those who never built community now in small major-specific cohorts. Boomer Bot checks on students regardless of credit hours for engagement and isolation — a proactive touchpoint exists but is bot-based, not human-to-human (confirmed). No institutional recognition exists for non-campus forms of leadership such as caregiving, community work, or off-campus employment (confirmed gap, June 2026).
Career & Future Self
Career fairs and internships increasingly urgent. Graduate school pathway entirely self-initiated for most. High-achieving first-gen students unlikely to consider grad school without an institutional prompt.

⚑ Stage 5 Gap Summary
Financial Access
Students beyond Year 4 may face reduced or eliminated aid eligibility. Financial holds can block graduation clearance even for academically ready students.
Academic Foundation
Post-75-hr advising gap lands here: some students discover missing requirements only at graduation check — too late without adding semesters. Exit exam at 102 hrs is data collection only, not student support.
Belonging & Engagement
Commencement and transition. Commencement info communicated via graduation application and a dedicated Brightspace community (confirmed). Participation tracked at aggregate level only — no individual-level follow-up for students who do not attend (confirmed gap). Culturally specific graduation celebrations not documented. Students who never found belonging remain invisible at completion.
Health & Basic Needs
CC "What's Next?" support confirmed. Abrupt loss of campus wellness resources at graduation; community mental health waitlists limit continuity of care.
Career & Future Self
Graduate Outcomes Survey: ~22% response rate; demographic non-response unanalyzed — data blind spot for first-gen, Black, and Pell-eligible graduates at precisely the moment the institution most needs visibility.
